About Us

Who We Are

Over 100 years of empowering voters and defending democracy across Rhode Island — rooted in the suffrage movement and committed to a future where every voice counts.

Our Story

A Century of Civic Empowerment

The League of Women Voters of Rhode Island is a nonpartisan, grassroots organization dedicated to empowering voters and defending democracy. Through education, advocacy, and civic engagement, LWVRI works to ensure that every Rhode Islander can participate fully in the democratic process.

The Providence League was chartered in 1919, very shortly after the League of Women Voters was created as a division of the National American Women Suffrage Association at the NAWSA convention in St. Louis, Missouri. It is one of the oldest leagues in the country.

Although Rhode Island is small, it was not unknown nationally during the fight for Suffrage. The state was home to pioneering figures including Paulina Wright Davis, Elizabeth Buffum Chace, Julia Ward Howe, and Alva Vanderbilt Belmont.

Today LWVRI brings together three local leagues — Providence, Newport County, and South County — along with statewide programs including the Book Club, Observer Corps, and education fund grants.

History

Key Milestones

1919

LWVRI Founded

The Providence League is chartered shortly after the NAWSA convention in St. Louis creates the national LWV.

1920

19th Amendment Ratified

Women gain the constitutional right to vote. Rhode Island's suffragists had fought for decades for this moment.

2020

Mail-In Ballot Victory

LWVRI fights to protect Rhode Island officials' ability to waive mail-in ballot security measures during COVID-19, upheld by the Supreme Court.

2022

Civics Education Victory

LWVRI files amicus brief in Cook v. McKee; case ends with agreement to strengthen civics education statewide.

2024

DEI Policy Adopted

LWVUS adopts a new Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion Policy, reaffirming the League's commitment to belonging for all.

2026

100+ Years Strong

LWVRI continues its century-long mission, mobilizing voters for the 2026 midterm elections and beyond.

RI Suffrage History

Rhode Island's Suffragists

Although Rhode Island is small, it was not unknown nationally during the fight for Women's Suffrage.

✊
Paulina Wright Davis

Pioneering women's rights advocate and editor of Una. Organized the first national women's rights convention in 1850.

🕊️
Elizabeth Buffum Chace

Abolitionist and women's suffrage leader from Valley Falls, RI. Active in the Underground Railroad and the New England suffrage movement.

✍️
Julia Ward Howe

Author of "The Battle Hymn of the Republic" and co-founder of the New England Woman Suffrage Association.

🏛️
Alva Vanderbilt Belmont

A major financier of the suffrage movement who campaigned in Rhode Island and nationally for the cause.

Our Commitment
Diversity, Equity & Inclusion

LWVRI is committed to fostering, cultivating, and preserving a culture of diversity, equity, inclusion, access, and belonging for all people. The League respects and values the richness of our community and ensures all people feel welcomed, respected, and empowered to participate fully.

Aligned with the LWVUS DEI Policy updated January 21, 2024.
