Local Leagues

Serving Communities Statewide

Rhode Island is a small state and all League activities are open to every member, regardless of local league affiliation. Three active leagues cover the Ocean State.

Providence & Surrounding Area
LWV Providence
Est. 1919

One of the oldest leagues in the country, chartered in 1919 shortly after the LWV was created at the NAWSA convention in St. Louis, MO. A century-long force for civic engagement in Rhode Island.

The Providence League leadership meets on the first Wednesday of odd-numbered months at 9:30 a.m. at One Richmond Square, Suite 220 A-W, Providence, RI 02906.

Newport County
LWV Newport County

The Newport County League brings civic engagement to one of Rhode Island's most historic regions. Known for organizing meaningful community events, democracy vigils, and voter education programs.

Among their notable activities, the Newport County League organized a January 6th vigil to commemorate Capitol events, honor police officers, and encourage civic involvement to safeguard democracy.

LWV South County

Covering southeastern Rhode Island's 10 towns, the South County League is known for dynamic programming, candidate forums, and voter engagement. They host the annual Susan B. Wilson Civic Merit Awards recognizing outstanding civic contributions.

The South County League co-sponsors events with local theaters and libraries — documentary screenings, author talks, and public discussions — alongside robust voter registration and GOTV activities.

Statewide Activities

Programs Open to All Members

📖 Book Club

The LWVRI Book Club meets regularly to discuss books related to democracy, civic engagement, women's history, and social justice. Open to all league members statewide — a great way to connect and deepen your understanding of the issues we care about.

Learn More →

🔍 Observer Corps

The Observer Corps attends and monitors public government meetings — legislative hearings, school committee meetings, city council sessions — ensuring open government and public accountability. Members write reports shared with the broader membership. Contact Liz Head to join.
