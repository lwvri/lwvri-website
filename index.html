Our Mission

Built on Over a Century of Civic Service

The League of Women Voters of Rhode Island is a nonpartisan, grassroots organization dedicated to empowering voters and defending democracy. Through education, advocacy, and civic engagement, LWVRI works to ensure that every Rhode Islander can participate fully in the democratic process.

LWVRI is committed to building and sustaining a culture where diversity, equity, inclusion, access, and belonging are not just principles, but lived practices. We honor and value the richness of our community and our members.

The Providence League was chartered in 1919, shortly after the LWV was created at the NAWSA convention in St. Louis. Rhode Island played a nationally significant role in the suffrage movement through Paulina Wright Davis, Elizabeth Buffum Chace, and Julia Ward Howe.

Advocacy & Positions

Issues We Champion

LWVRI takes positions and advocates on key issues affecting democracy, civil rights, and quality of life in Rhode Island.

🗳️
Voting Rights

Protecting and expanding ballot access for all eligible voters, opposing restrictive measures, and promoting fair election administration.

Advocating for strong environmental protections and supporting climate action at state and federal levels, including clean water rules.

Local Leagues

Serving Communities Statewide

Rhode Island is a small state and League activities are open to all members regardless of local league affiliation.

LWV Providence
Providence & Surrounding Area

One of the oldest leagues in the country, chartered in 1919. Hosts the Conversation Series, Lippitt House Discussions, advocates for library funding, and maintains an Observer Corps.

Our Commitment
Diversity, Equity & Inclusion

LWVRI is committed to fostering, cultivating, and preserving a culture of diversity, equity, inclusion, access, and belonging for all people. We honor and value the richness of our community — and work to ensure all people feel welcomed, respected, and empowered to participate fully in democracy.
